# Hero Graphic Design Prompt: Constellation Score Path

## What I need
A hero graphic for the landing page of an SAT prep quiz funnel. This is the single visual element on page 1 — it needs to communicate "your child's score can go from here to here over 12 weeks" without any words beyond two score numbers.

## The concept
A constellation-style path that shows an SAT score climbing from 1180 to 1450 over 12 weeks. Each dot represents a week. The dots are connected by lines, forming an upward path from bottom-left to top-right. Surrounding the main path are subtle scattered dots and faint connecting lines that create a constellation/star map texture in the background — this is core to the brand identity (illuminairy = illuminate + luminary, the brand metaphor is "lighting the path").

## Specifications

**Structure:**
- 12 dots along the main path, one per week
- Path climbs from bottom-left (1180) to top-right (1450)
- The climb should NOT be a straight diagonal — it should have a natural, slightly organic upward trajectory with subtle variation (scores don't go up perfectly linearly)
- Score label "1180" on the first dot
- Score label "1450" on the last dot
- No other score labels on intermediate dots
- No week labels needed

**Color (from brand palette — flat colors only, no gradients):**
- Background: white (#ffffff) — this sits on a white landing page
- Starting dot: tomato (#f24822) — signals "problem, you are here"
- Ending dot: green (#2f8b55) — signals "goal, destination reached"
- Intermediate dots along the path: black (#000000) or dark gray, small
- Path connecting lines: black or dark gray, thin/crisp
- Background constellation scatter dots: very light gray or faint, barely visible — just enough to create star-field texture without competing with the main path
- Background constellation connecting lines: even fainter than the dots, hairline thin
- Score labels ("1180" and "1450"): black, bold, clean typography

**Style:**
- Sharp, geometric, precise — no rounded softness
- Mid-century modern meets data visualization meets star chart
- Editorial and minimal — this should feel like it belongs in a premium magazine or a museum exhibit, not in an edtech app
- The overall composition should feel like a night sky star chart but rendered in a clean, daytime, minimal aesthetic
- No decorative elements. No icons. No illustrations. Just dots, lines, and two numbers.

**Dimensions:**
- Design at high resolution but it needs to work at roughly 350px wide on mobile (this is a mobile-first landing page, ~390px viewport)
- Should also look good at larger sizes for desktop and for use in social media ads

## What this should NOT look like
- A generic line chart or graph
- An edtech app screenshot
- A before/after score card (every competitor uses those)
- Anything with rounded, bubbly, or "friendly" aesthetics
- Anything with gradients, glows, or drop shadows (except possibly a very subtle glow on the green endpoint dot)

## Context
This graphic sits on the landing page directly below the headline "High GPA, low SAT?" and subheadline "We'll help you figure out why they're struggling." and directly above the CTA button "Get my answers →". A mom scrolling Instagram at 10pm taps an ad, lands on this page, and needs to understand this graphic in under 2 seconds. The graphic should make her feel: "oh — they can take my kid from here to there."
